About the Workshop

The GigaBrain Challenge 2026 aims to push the boundaries of embodied intelligence. We invite researchers and practitioners to explore cutting-edge topics including Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models, Embodied Foundation Models, World Models, and Real2Sim2Real transfer learning.

This workshop will feature invited talks, paper presentations, and a rigorous competition across three tracks. We are accepting paper submissions on relevant topics and will host approximately 20 poster boards for selected works.

Note:

The competition will conclude by May 15, 2026. No onsite competition will be held at CVPR; the workshop session will focus on awards ceremonies and presentations by winning teams.

Paper Submissions

Call for Papers

We welcome submissions related to VLA, embodied foundation models, world models, and Real2Sim2Real / Sim2Real2Sim. Accepted papers will be presented in the workshop and selected works will be assigned poster boards.

Competition Tracks

Track 1

RoboTwin Simulation

Based on the RoboTwin simulation environment. This track focuses on complex, dexterous, and long-horizon tasks in diverse simulation scenarios.

Timeline:

Mid-March to Mid-April

Track 2

World Model Challenge

Focused on the quality and consistency of generated data and its potential as an evaluator (RL integration). GigaAI will provide World Model baselines and post-training data.

Timeline:

Mid-March to Mid-April

Track 3

RoboChallenge Real Robot

Invite-only track featuring winners from Track 1 & 2 alongside top academic/industry teams (~10-15 teams). 10 submission chances per team. Multi-task strategy required (one model for all tasks).

Timeline:

Mid-April to Mid-May

Important Dates

Mid-March

Competition Kickoff

Track 1 (Simulation) and Track 2 (World Model) begin.

Mid-April

Phase 1 Conclusion & Phase 2 Start

Track 1 & 2 conclude. Track 3 (Real Robot) begins with invited teams.

May 15

Competition Ends

All tracks conclude. Final rankings determined.

June 3-7

CVPR 2026 Workshop
